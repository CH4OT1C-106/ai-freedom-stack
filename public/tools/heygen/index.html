What is HeyGen?

An AI platform that generates talking-head videos from text scripts. Pick a stock avatar (or create a clone of yourself), paste your script, and it produces a video of the avatar speaking with lip sync. Includes translation features for multilingual content.

Who is it for?

  • Best for: Course creators, SaaS companies needing product demos, anyone doing multilingual video content
  • Not for: Solo YouTube creators (audiences prefer authentic presence over AI avatars)

How we’d actually use it

Creating a product demo without being on camera:

  1. Write a script for your tool walkthrough
  2. Pick an avatar that matches your brand
  3. Upload screen recordings as B-roll
  4. HeyGen generates the talking-head segments
  5. Edit together in your video editor

Honest take: For most solo builders, recording yourself on a phone + ElevenLabs for voiceover is cheaper and more authentic. HeyGen makes sense at scale.

What’s good

  • No camera or video skills needed
  • Multilingual translation is the killer feature
  • Good for batch-producing training/demo videos
  • Script-to-video workflow is genuinely fast
  • Custom avatar clones are getting better

What’s not

  • Avatars still look artificial â€” uncanny valley is real
  • Expensive per minute of content
  • Audiences are increasingly skeptical of AI avatars
  • Lock-in: your avatar videos only work within HeyGen
  • Custom clones have inconsistent quality

FAQ

Q: Should I use HeyGen for my YouTube channel? A: Probably not. YouTube audiences value authenticity. An AI avatar hosting your channel will hurt more than help. Use it for product demos and course content instead.

Q: HeyGen or Synthesia? A: Similar products. HeyGen has better avatar quality. Synthesia has more enterprise features. For solo builders, neither is essential.
